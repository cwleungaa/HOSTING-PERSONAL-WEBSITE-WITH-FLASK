Uncertainty

This section is about how we can create AI that makes optimal decisions given limited information and uncertainty.
We use probability theory to handle the uncertainty.
It introduces:

The basic Probability rules with Unconditional Probability and Conditional Probability.

Bayesian Networks -- a data structure that represents the dependencies among random variables.

Markov Models -- with the assumption that the current state depends on only a finite fixed number of previous states,
to get a more rough approximation of the probabilities of interest.

Project:

1. PageRank
Write an AI to rank web pages by importance.
https://cs50.harvard.edu/ai/2020/projects/2/pagerank/

A more important website means more other websites link to the current one.
We calculate the importance with two approaches:
By sampling pages from a Markov Chain random surfer and by iteratively applying the PageRank formula.


2. Heredity
Write an AI to assess the likelihood that a person will have a particular genetic trait.
https://cs50.harvard.edu/ai/2020/projects/2/heredity/

We use Bayesian Networks to build the relationship between parents and child.
Then, we use this model to make inferences about a population.
